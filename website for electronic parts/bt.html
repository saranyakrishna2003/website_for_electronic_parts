BATTERY

An electric battery is a source of electric power consisting of one or more electrochemical cells with external connections for powering electrical devices.When a battery is supplying power, its positive terminal is the cathode and its negative terminal is the anode.The terminal marked negative is the source of electrons that will flow through an external electric circuit to the positive terminal. When a battery is connected to an external electric load, a redox reaction converts high-energy reactants to lower-energy products, and the free-energy difference is delivered to the external circuit as electrical energy.
